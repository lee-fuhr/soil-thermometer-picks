Quick answer

Soil temperature is one of the most overlooked factors in successful gardening – seeds germinate poorly, seedlings stunt, and roots rot when the ground is too cold or too warm. A good soil thermometer takes the guesswork out of planting times and helps you make the most of your growing season. When choosing a soil thermometer, pay attention to the length of the stem (longer stems reach deeper into beds or pots), the temperature range (most cover 0–180°F), and whether you prefer an analog dial or a digital display. Accuracy matters, but so does durability: a waterproof body and a sturdy probe that won’t bend in compacted soil are worth paying extra for.
