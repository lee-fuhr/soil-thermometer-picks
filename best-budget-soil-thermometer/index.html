Getting the soil temperature right before planting is one of those small steps that makes a big difference. Seeds that go into cold, wet ground often rot; seeds that go into warm enough soil germinate faster and grow stronger. A good soil thermometer takes the guesswork out of that decision, and you don’t need to spend much to get one that works well. When you’re shopping on a budget, look for a clear, easy-to-read dial or digital display, a probe long enough to reach root depth (at least four inches), and a sturdy stem that won’t bend. Temperature range matters too, you want something that reads from about 20°F to 120°F. The three picks below cover the essentials without unnecessary frills, and each suits a slightly different style of gardening.
